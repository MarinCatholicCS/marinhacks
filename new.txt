About
Can you ship in 12 hours?
MarinHacks is Marin Catholic's first hackathon. Open to middle and high school students across the Bay Area. Come with an idea, leave with a project, and compete for prizes alongside hundreds of fellow builders.
We are trying to bring hackathons into Marin County and encourage a greater STEM involvement in our community.
John Paul II Student Center · Marin Catholic, Kentfield CA
Central Theme
Your projects will center around this on-word theme.
Catered Food
Lunch and dinner provided. Stay fueled. Stay focused.
Real Prizes
Compete for gift cards, track-specific awards including scholarships and sponsor credits, and tool access.
All Skill Levels
Beginners welcome. Solo or team. You may form groups day of.
Schedule
Day of the event
A full day of building and competing. Get ready.
8:00 AM
Check-in
Arrive and find a seat
Check in at the entrance of the JPII Student Center. Remember to bring your signed participant waiver. Feel free to get your workspace set up while you wait for the opening ceremony.
8:15 AM
Welcome Presentation
Opening remarks and introductions
Kick off MarinHacks with opening remarks from the organizing team. We will introduce our staff, sponsors, judges, and walk through everything you need to know for the day.
8:30 AM
Theme Reveal & Start
Theme announced — hacking begins!
The overall theme and track categories will be revealed. Immediately following the announcement, hacking officially begins — good luck!
9:00 AM
Nico Zametto's Workshop
"How to Hackathon"
Nico Zametto, 4x hackathon winner and winner of a Y Combinator hackathon, will lead an interactive workshop on how to approach and excel at hackathons. From concept to competition, learn how to scope your idea, build efficiently under time pressure, and deliver a compelling demo.
10:00 AM
Chess Tournament
Step away from the keyboard and compete
Need a mental reset? Join our single-elimination blitz chess tournament running throughout the day. Games are 5 minutes each. The winner takes home some MarinHacks swag.
12:00 PM
Lunch
Catered lunch
Take a break and enjoy a catered lunch featuring assorted sandwiches and salad. A great opportunity to step away from your project, meet other participants, and recharge for the afternoon.
1:00 PM
Boba Break
Complimentary boba courtesy of Sharetea
Courtesy of our local sponsor Sharetea, participants who selected a boba drink during registration can pick up their free drink. A small perk to keep the energy going.
3:00 PM
"Ditch That Agent!"
Old-school coding challenge, no AI allowed
Think you can code without your AI assistant? In this bracket-style competition, go head-to-head against fellow participants to see who can write a correct algorithm the fastest - on paper. A fun reminder the fundamentals still matter.
6:00 PM
Submissions Due
All projects must be submitted by 6:00 PM
All projects must be submitted to DevPost by 6:00 PM sharp. Any commits to your repository after the deadline will result in immediate disqualification. You are welcome to continue refining your presentation and demo after submission.
6:10 PM
Dinner
Pizza served before judging begins
Fuel up before you present. Pizza will be served while teams prepare for their judging slots.
6:30 PM
Judging Begins
Teams present to a panel of judges
Teams will be called in scheduled order to present their projects to a panel of three judges. Be prepared to walk through your project, explain your process, and demo what you built.
7:30 PM
Geoguessr & Trivia
Open activities while results are tallied
Hosted by Alex Willard, all participants are welcome to join a Geoguessr tournament and trivia rounds while the judges finalize scores and deliberate on winners.
8:00 PM
Closing Ceremony
Winners announced and closing remarks
Join us for the closing ceremony as we announce the winners, hand out awards, and wrap up the day. Regardless of the outcome, take pride in what you built - creating something from nothing in 12 hours is no small feat.

FAQ
Common questions
Who can participate?
MarinHacks is open to high school students who are 18 or younger on August 2nd, the day of the event. Qualified middle school students may appeal once the application is available. All skill levels are welcomed, though we recommend that you come in with a basic knowledge of programming and project management.
Is it free to attend?
Yes! MarinHacks is completely free to attend. We will provide catered meals, snacks, and all the resources you need to build your project.
What should I bring?
You should bring a portable laptop, charger, drinks, and snacks. Please do not bring anything too large (ex. External Monitors, 3D Printers). If you have any questions of what to bring, contact us through email or Discord.
Can I work alone or do I need a team?
Yes, you can absolutely work alone! However, we highly encourage you find and work with a team. You learn many valuable lessons through code collaboration and version control.
Where is MarinHacks hosted?
MarinHacks will be held at the Marin Catholic's John Paul II Student Center.
View the campus map
What kind of projects can I build?
Anything! Websites, software, mobile apps, etc. Hardware Hacks are allowed, but are discouraged since no pre-built hardware projects are allowed and because of the short working time
Is there a theme?
Yes, there is a theme and it will be revealed during the opening ceremony. All projects, even those in different tracks, must relate to the theme.
What are the prizes?
Top teams will be awarded gift cards - $250 for 1st place, $150 for 2nd, and $100 for 3rd. Beyond the overall prizes, each track will feature its own dedicated awards, including research program scholarships and credits from our sponsor companies. All registered participants will receive access to a curated set of sponsor tools throughout the event.
